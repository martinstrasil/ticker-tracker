Latest Earnings & Delivery Update

On January 22, 2026, Intel reported Q4 2025 revenue of $13.7 billion (down 4% YoY; not adjusted for Altera deconsolidation) with non-GAAP EPS of $0.15 vs. GAAP EPS of $(0.12). Full-year 2025 revenue was $52.9 billion (flat YoY) with $9.7 billion operating cash flow. Management guided Q1 2026 revenue of $11.7B–$12.7B, non-GAAP EPS of $0.00 at the revenue midpoint, and flagged lowest available supply in Q1 improving through Q2+ (CFO commentary). Product delivery milestones include Intel 18A high-volume manufacturing in the U.S. and the Core Ultra Series 3 AI PC platform ramp. Next catalyst: April 23, 2026 Q1 results (after market close; webcast per Intel IR calendar).

▼ Risks

  • Q1 supply trough — revenue/EPS guide implies constrained shipments and weak seasonality
  • Foundry economics — scale losses until external customers ramp meaningfully
  • Client weakness — CCG revenue −7% YoY in Q4 highlights PC cycle exposure
  • Competitive intensity — AMD, Arm, and custom silicon cap pricing power in key sockets

Bottom Line

Intel is simultaneously executing a credible technology story (18A, AI PC, data-center CPUs) and asking investors to underwrite a volatile transition: GAAP earnings remain messy, Q1 is explicitly supply-gated, and the equity has re-rated aggressively into that uncertainty. Long-term bulls are paying a tactical premium; bears focus on forward multiple stretch and the gap between narrative and near-term EPS power.
