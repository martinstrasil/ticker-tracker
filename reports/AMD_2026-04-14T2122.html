Latest Earnings & Delivery Update — Q4 FY2025 (reported Feb 2026)

AMD closed FY2025 at a record $34.6B revenue (+34% YoY) with Q4 non‑GAAP EPS of $1.53 on ~$10.27B revenue, ahead of the pre-release consensus (~$1.32 EPS). Data Center revenue reached ~$5.4B in the quarter (+39% YoY), reflecting continued EPYC strength and accelerating Instinct traction (management highlighted MI350 momentum and roadmap visibility into rack-scale Helios / MI450 later in 2026). Gross margin expanded to 57% non‑GAAP in Q4. For Q1 2026, AMD guided revenue to about $9.8B (±$0.3B) with non‑GAAP gross margin around 55% — a sequential revenue step that is seasonally consistent but keeps the narrative sensitive to “AI growth vs digestion” headlines. Watch the next call for Instinct shipment linearity, China export assumptions, and embedded/gaming mix.

▲ Catalysts

  • Data Center share of wallet — EPYC wins and rising Instinct attach in training/inference buildouts
  • MI350 / CDNA4 cadence — denser AI SKU stack vs prior gen improves ASPs where supply permits
  • Gross margin lift — richer mix and manufacturing leverage as DC scales
  • Enterprise AI budgets — second-source demand from hyperscalers diversifying accelerators
  • Software progress — ROCm + open ecosystem partnerships reducing friction vs incumbent CUDA dominance
  • Balance sheet optionality — capacity to fund R&D and strategic M&A without distress

▼ Risks

  • NVIDIA competition — faster Blackwell/GB200-class ramps can compress AMD GPU pricing power
  • Valuation sensitivity — high multiples punish any growth deceleration or guide miss
  • Export controls — geopolitical rules can delay Instinct revenue recognition and distort margins via charges
  • Inventory cycles — GPU digestion phases can spike working capital and dent FCF optics
  • Client/Embedded softness — PC refresh timing and industrial demand can dilute “pure AI” narrative
  • Execution on Helios/MI450 — rack-scale ramps concentrate timing risk into late 2026

Bottom Line

AMD is executing on the right vector: record revenues, expanding Data Center mix, and improving non‑GAAP gross margins. The pushback is price: the market is already paying for a multi‑year AI compute share‑gain story, so the error band is narrower — good news gets partially discounted, bad news gets amplified. With sell‑side sentiment clustered around Moderate Buy and a consensus price target near $290, the setup favors disciplined accumulation (scale in on volatility) rather than chasing strength blindly. Risk is moderate‑to‑elevated mainly due to multiples and competitive dynamics, not solvency.
