Latest Earnings & Delivery Context

Q4 FY25 (reported Jan 26, 2026): Revenue $33.5B, adjusted EPS $0.52, adjusted EBITDA $11.2B, and free cash flow $4.2B. Full-year consolidated revenue reached $125.6B with $16.6B free cash flow — management indicated guidance was met or exceeded across consolidated targets. Consumer broadband growth was highlighted as the strongest in a decade, with fiber momentum continuing. Q1 FY26 results are scheduled for April 22, 2026 (before NYSE open, call 8:30 a.m. ET) — the first quarter under refreshed segment disclosure (Advanced Connectivity, Legacy, Latin America) following the Lumen fiber close (Feb 2, 2026) and EchoStar spectrum licensing, so look-through on fiber ARPU, legacy erosion pace, and integration costs will dominate the narrative.

▲ Catalysts

  • Fiber scale + ARPU — continued AT&T Fiber net adds and converged offers support stable broadband economics.
  • Wireless service revenue strength — multi-year highs in service revenue growth cited by management through FY25.
  • FCF & capital returns — elevated FY25 FCF underpins ~4% yield and the communicated $45B+ shareholder return framework for 2026–2028.
  • Cost discipline / AI ops — productivity programs and network automation can defend margins against inflation.
  • Strategic assets — spectrum licenses and fiber footprint improve long-term capacity for 5G & fixed wireless convergence.
  • Multiple re-rating — low-teens P/E leaves room for modest expansion if FY26 EPS hits the $2.25–$2.35 guided band.

▼ Risks

  • Leverage overhang — net debt ~$129B keeps AT&T sensitive to rates, spreads, and M&A execution risk.
  • Legacy wireline drag — Business Wireline declines can offset connectivity gains if erosion accelerates.
  • Integration complexity — Lumen fiber + EchoStar spectrum increase execution and capex intensity through 2026.
  • Competitive intensity — cable fiber overbuild, T-Mobile fixed wireless, and price wars could pressure ARPU.
  • Regulatory / legal — pole-access disputes, lead-sheath remediation overhang, and recurring legal items remain headline risks.
  • Macro sensitivity — consumer stress or B2B slowdown would flow quickly into churn, financing costs, and capex flexibility.

Bottom Line

AT&T offers a rare combination of high single-digit earnings multiples, a cash-funded dividend, and improving connectivity mix (mobility + fiber) — but the investment case still hinges on managing net debt while digesting 2026 integrations and new segment reporting. Risk is moderate: balance-sheet weight offsets attractive valuation and cash returns. Income-oriented holders can stay the course into the April 22 print; growth investors should treat T as a bond-proxy equity with capped upside unless service-revenue acceleration surprises persist.
