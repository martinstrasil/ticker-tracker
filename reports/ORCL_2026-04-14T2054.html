Latest Earnings & Delivery Update

Q3 FY26 (ended Feb 28, 2026) — Oracle reported $17.19B revenue (+22% YoY) vs. ~$16.91B consensus, and non-GAAP EPS $1.79 vs. $1.70 expected. Cloud revenue reached $8.9B (+44%), with cloud infrastructure (IaaS) $4.9B (+84%). RPO jumped to ~$553B (+325% YoY). Management raised the FY27 revenue outlook by $1B to ~$90B and guided Q4 non-GAAP EPS $1.92–$1.96 on ~19–20% revenue growth. The company also outlined up to $45–50B in calendar-2026 financing to expand AI capacity while noting customer prepayments and bring-your-own-GPU structures can limit incremental borrowing. Media volatility around the Abilene Stargate campus was followed by Oracle stating two buildings are live and the remainder is on track. Next major print: Q4 FY26 (~mid-June 2026) — focus on capex cadence, FCF trajectory, and RPO conversion.

▲ Catalysts

  • Record AI backlog — RPO near $553B underpins multi-year revenue visibility if execution holds.
  • IaaS re-acceleration — +84% YoY in Q3 signals capacity coming online for hyperscale workloads.
  • FY27 guide lift — ~$90B revenue target resets Street scale assumptions for cloud mix.
  • Customer-funded models — prepayments and supplier leases can improve cash timing vs. worst-case debt models.
  • Database multicloud tailwind — Oracle positions OCI + Autonomous DB as neutral layer across AWS, Azure, and GCP.
  • AI agent suite expansion — new automation SKUs across ERP/HCM/CRM can lift SaaS attach over time.

▼ Risks

  • Negative FCF — ~$13B TTM cash burn as Oracle outspends depreciation to win AI land grabs.
  • Financing dilution — large equity/debt programs could pressure EPS if cloud yields lag.
  • Concentration — outsized commitments from a handful of AI customers raise renewal/cliff risk.
  • Competitive cap intensity — AWS, Azure, and GCP can match price/performance on GPUs and regions.
  • Execution / optics — data-center headlines (e.g., Abilene) can whipsaw sentiment even when fundamentals beat.
  • Margin mix — renting GPUs is structurally lower margin than classic database software — multiple compression risk if growth slows.

Bottom Line

Oracle is one of the clearest AI-infrastructure beneficiaries in software, with cloud re-acceleration and a staggering RPO book that supports a bullish multi-year revenue path. The offset is balance-sheet stress: heavy capex, deeply negative free cash flow, and large capital raises mean the equity behaves like a levered play on AI demand. After a >50% drawdown from the 2025 peak, valuation is no longer euphoric on forward earnings, but the stock will remain volatile around financing headlines and quarter-to-quarter FCF. Position sizing should reflect financing and customer-concentration tail risks, not just top-line momentum.
