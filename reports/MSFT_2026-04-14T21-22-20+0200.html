Latest Earnings & Delivery Update — FY26 Q2 (Dec quarter)

On January 28, 2026, Microsoft reported $81.3B revenue (+17% YoY), $38.3B operating income (+21%), and $5.16 diluted GAAP EPS (+60% YoY; includes non-recurring tax benefit dynamics called out in filings). Microsoft Cloud reached $51.5B (+26% YoY), with Azure growth of 39% and commercial remaining performance obligation surging 110% to $625B—a demand signal for forward workloads. Offsetting enthusiasm: More Personal Computing revenue declined 3%, gross margin compressed slightly above 68% on AI infrastructure mix, and CapEx plus finance leases landed at $37.5B as the company accelerates datacenter capacity. Shares traded off after hours on margin-path and spend intensity questions despite headline beats—classic “good numbers, noisy price” dynamics for hyperscaler AI cycles.

▲ Catalysts

  • Azure re-acceleration — 39% YoY growth reasserts MSFT as default enterprise AI runtime
  • $625B commercial RPO — multi-year visibility into cloud & M365 seat expansion
  • Copilot monetization — 15M+ paid Copilot seats signal attach into existing enterprise TAM
  • Operator / agentic stack — GitHub Copilot + Azure AI Studio deepen developer lock-in
  • OpenAI partnership optionality — API traffic & capacity sharing remain structurally accretive
  • Defense & sovereign clouds — regulated verticals expand ASPs and contract duration
  • FY26 Q3 guide reset — clean guide on Apr ~30 could de-risk the capex narrative into H2

▼ Risks

  • AI capex treadmill — $37.5B quarterly spend pace raises ROI timing & FCF absorption questions
  • Gross-margin glide path — sub-70% company gross margin may persist while GPU estates scale
  • Regulatory / antitrust — Teams bundling, Azure/OpenAI linkage, and M365 pricing remain scrutiny vectors
  • Consumer segment softness — MPC -3% shows cyclical PC exposure & gaming volatility
  • Hyperscaler competition — AWS & GCP price/feature wars could compress incremental cloud yields
  • Power & permitting constraints — datacenter delivery risk in key metros could slow revenue conversion
  • Macro & FX — strong USD and enterprise budget pauses could temper FY27 upside

Bottom Line

Microsoft remains one of the cleanest large-cap expressions of durable enterprise software + hyperscale cloud, with Azure and Microsoft 365 forming a high-switching-cost stack. The 2026 share drawdown is less about broken growth than about capital intensity and margin pathing in the AI build-out phase. At a mid-20s P/E versus recent historical averages, risk is skewed toward execution variance on capex returns, not solvency. For long-term holders, accumulation windows often align with these “capex wall” debates—provided Azure consumption stays resilient through FY26 Q3 guidance.
