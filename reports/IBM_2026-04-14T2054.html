Latest Update — Q4 2025 Results & Q1 2026 Catalyst

IBM reported Q4 2025 revenue of $19.7B (~+12% YoY reported; ~+9% constant currency), ahead of consensus near $19.2B. Adjusted EPS reached $4.52, topping the ~$4.32 Street estimate. Infrastructure sales jumped on mainframe demand; software grew double digits; consulting growth remained modest. Management guided >5% constant-currency revenue growth for FY2026 and about $1B incremental free cash flow versus 2025’s record $14.7B. The generative-AI related book of business crossed $12.5B cumulative. Next major catalyst: April 22, 2026 Q1 earnings webcast (5:00 p.m. ET) — focus on Red Hat/watsonx traction, consulting bookings, mainframe cycle cadence, and updated FCF cadence.

Bottom Line

IBM is no longer a “declining mainframe” story — FY25 showed the strongest revenue growth in years, record free cash flow, and a tangible Gen-AI revenue pipeline. Valuation is closer to fair than frothy, and the balance sheet supports dividends plus investment. The bear case is cyclical: hardware strength normalizes, consulting wobbles, and AI competition compresses pricing power. For long-term holders, the risk skew is moderate: fundamentals improved, but the stock needs consistent proof each quarter that software + platform growth can offset hardware normalization.
