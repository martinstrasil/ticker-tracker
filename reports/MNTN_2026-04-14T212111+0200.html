Latest Earnings & Delivery / Guidance Update

On February 10, 2026, MNTN reported record Q4 and full-year 2025 results: Q4 revenue $87.1M (~+25% YoY GAAP), gross margin 82%, and adjusted EBITDA $28.1M (32% of revenue). FY25 revenue reached $290.1M (+29% YoY GAAP) with adjusted EBITDA of $68.0M (23% of revenue). Management guided FY26 revenue of $345–355M and adjusted EBITDA of $94.6–99.6M, implying continued operating leverage. Q1’26 revenue guidance was set at $71.3–73.3M (midpoint ~+22% YoY vs. adjusted Q1’25). There is no separate vehicle delivery metric; key demand proxy is Performance TV customer growth (3,632; +63% YoY). The next quarterly report is expected the first week of May 2026 (vendor calendars cluster around May 4–11, 2026).

▲ Catalysts

  • Operating leverage — FY26 EBITDA guide ~$95–100M embeds ~350–400 bps margin expansion vs. FY25.
  • Platform scale — PTV customer count +63% YoY evidences durable land-and-expand motion.
  • Creative / measurement stack — Creative-as-a-Subscription and closed-loop attribution deepen net revenue retention.
  • Multiple re-rating — shares trade far below sell-side PTs if margins stabilize above 30%.
  • M&A optionality — profitable SaaS + clean narrative could attract strategic interest in ad-automation.

▼ Risks

  • Macro ad cyclicality — SMB performance marketing budgets are first to tighten in a slowdown.
  • Competitive intensity — The Trade Desk, Meta/Google duopoly, and retail media networks compress pricing power.
  • Volatility & liquidity — ~72% drawdown from 52w high; elevated sentiment beta on small revenue misses.
  • GAAP vs. adjusted gap — FY25 still carried net loss and one-time IPO costs — adjusted metrics need to convert to durable GAAP profits.
  • Guidance execution — FY26 top-line acceleration is already priced as base case; misses would de-rate aggressively.

Bottom Line

MNTN is a high-conviction growth / high-volatility name: the 2025–2026 fundamental story improved materially (revenue scale, gross margin >80%, EBITDA inflection, explicit FY26 targets), but the chart remains wrecked relative to the 52-week high, reflecting prior multiple compression and small-cap risk appetite. The bull path is straightforward — hit $350M+ revenue with ~28–32% EBITDA margins and sustain customer adds — which could close a large chunk of the ~$22 consensus PT gap. The bear path is equally clear: any ad recession, competitive share loss, or guide cut given thin GAAP profitability would extend the drawdown. Position sizing should reflect equity volatility, not just operating quality.
