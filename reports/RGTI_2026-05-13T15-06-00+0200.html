Latest Earnings / Delivery Update

Rigetti reported Q1 2026 results (press release, May 11, 2026): revenue $4.4M (+199% YoY; beating consensus of $4.09M), with non-GAAP diluted EPS of $(0.04) beating estimates of $(0.05). GAAP net income of $33.1M was driven by a non-cash gain on derivative warrant liabilities (fair-value decrease). Cash & investments stood at $569.0M with zero debt. Key delivery: the 108-qubit Cepheus-1-108Q system went into general availability across Rigetti QCS, Amazon Braket, Azure Quantum, and qBraid — validated as the industry's largest modular gate-based system in production. The company also completed delivery of a 9-qubit Novera QPU to the University of Saskatchewan and announced an intention to invest up to $100M in the UK toward a 1,000+ qubit system within 3–4 years.

▲ Catalysts

  • Tech execution — 108Q Cepheus-1 GA; 99.8% 2Q gate fidelity on 9Q with adiabatic CZ; 99.9% on prototypes
  • Balance sheet — $569M cash / no debt funds multi-year roadmap without further dilution
  • Q1'26 inflection — +199% YoY revenue; Novera on-prem demand returning; beat consensus
  • C-DAC & UK orders — $8.4M India PO (H2'26 deploy); up to $100M UK investment pipeline
  • Roadmap clarity — 150+ qubits at 99.7% by YE26; 1,000+ qubits at 99.8% by YE27
  • Fab-1 & chiplet moat — proprietary in-house fab + modular chiplet architecture for scaling

▼ Risks

  • Extreme multiple — P/S ~700× vs peers (IonQ ~85×); growth must accelerate orders of magnitude
  • Revenue scale — FY25 was $7.1M (−36% YoY); Q1'26 still only $4.4M; lumpy PO-driven
  • Dilution history — $350M ATM in Q2'25 nearly doubled share count (184M → 332M)
  • GAAP volatility — warrant fair-value swings dominate P&L (Q3'25: −$201M; Q1'25: +$43M)
  • Vol & drawdown — 52w range $9.85–$58.15 (6× span); −67% from ATH; high beta
  • Competition — IBM (1000+ qubits), Google (Willow), IonQ, neutral-atom / photonics players

Bottom Line

Rigetti offers one of the purest exposures to chiplet-based superconducting quantum computing, with technology milestones tracking to schedule and a fortress-class balance sheet that removes near-term survival risk. Q1'26 was a clear positive inflection — revenue reignited, the 108Q system went live across major clouds, and the non-GAAP loss narrowed. The problem remains valuation vs. scale: with TTM revenue under $10M supporting a ~$6.4B market cap, the equity prices in flawless multi-year execution with essentially no margin of error. Combined with fresh dilution memory and GAAP noise that can whipsaw quarterly prints, composite risk sits at 71/100. Skew: Hold / spec-sleeve only — size very small, use drawdowns for cost-averaging, and pair with diversified quantum/AI vehicles.
