Latest Earnings & Operations — Fiscal Q2 2026 (period ended Dec 31, 2025)

American Battery Technology Company reported record quarterly revenue of about $4.8M for its fiscal second quarter of 2026, with company commentary highlighting more revenue in that quarter than in the prior four quarters combined, and recycling throughput up roughly 70% quarter-over-quarter at its first commercial facility. Management also flagged a milestone where combined revenue and interest income exceeded cash cost of goods sold for the period, while cash and restricted cash were cited around $48.7M as of 2025-12-31 after ATM and warrant-related financing. Operating expenses remain large versus revenue; the next print should clarify gross-margin durability, opex cadence, and capex timing for the larger second-site program (including DOE-supported scope).

▲ Catalysts

  • Commercial ramp — first-facility throughput scaling; revenue inflection visible in Dec quarter.
  • Second recycling facility — management targeting materially higher capacity vs. plant one; DOE grant headlines (~$144M) as non-dilutive support if milestones hold.
  • Tonopah Flats lithium — PFS published (Oct 2025); EXIM LOI (~$900M) signals ambition on financing structure if project de-risks.
  • Strategic positioning — U.S. battery supply chain / recycling policy tailwinds; EPA-related recycling contract momentum (per trade press).
  • Liquidity window — strengthened cash post-financing extends runway for engineering and commissioning.

▼ Risks

  • Equity overhang — repeated ATM/warrant usage can cap rallies and dilute per-share economics.
  • Profitability gap — deep net losses and negative ROA/ROC until scale and pricing prove durable.
  • Execution / capex — second-site build is capital- and schedule-intensive; cost overruns would pressure funding.
  • Commodity & offtake — lithium price and black-mass economics can swing faster than operating improvements.
  • Volatility — 52-week range $1.02–$11.49 implies gap risk and sentiment-driven drawdowns unrelated to quarterlies.

Bottom Line

ABAT is transitioning from narrative to measurable commercial revenue in recycling, with a credible liquidity runway and meaningful non-dilutive funding optics around its next-wave projects. The equity, however, still prices substantial future execution: margins are not yet proven at scale, opex remains heavy, and the capital structure has relied on equity markets. Position sizing should reflect binary project and financing outcomes more than near-term GAAP earnings.
